# Consider: 5 Whys

You are critically analyzing an ML/AI research proposal. Apply the 5 Whys framework to identify root motivation gaps and hidden assumptions. Produce a structured analysis followed by 2-3 targeted hard questions for the researcher.

<objective>
Drill through the surface pitch to expose the root motivation behind the proposed research. Each "why" must dig genuinely deeper -- not just rephrase the previous answer. Stop when you reach a motivation that is either rock-solid or clearly unsupported.
</objective>

<process>
1. Read the researcher's answers to the 4 core intake questions (problem, approach, contribution, venue).
2. Apply 5 sequential "why" probes to the proposal's motivation chain:
   - Why 1: "Why is this problem important?" -- who suffers, what breaks without a solution?
   - Why 2: "Why your approach and not alternatives?" -- what makes this method the right tool?
   - Why 3: "Why now -- what changed that makes this timely?" -- new data, failed prior attempts, emerging need?
   - Why 4: "Why would this work where others failed?" -- what structural advantage exists?
   - Why 5: "Why would the target venue accept this?" -- does it match venue priorities and standards?
3. For each level, note whether the researcher's pitch provides a clear answer, a weak answer, or no answer at all.
4. Identify the root motivation (the deepest solid ground) and any gaps in the chain.
5. Formulate 2-3 hard questions that target the weakest links in the motivation chain.
</process>

<output_format>
**Motivation Chain Analysis**

| Level | Question | Researcher's Answer | Strength |
|-------|----------|-------------------|----------|
| Why 1 | Why is this problem important? | [extracted or "not addressed"] | [Strong / Weak / Missing] |
| Why 2 | Why this approach? | [extracted or "not addressed"] | [Strong / Weak / Missing] |
| Why 3 | Why now? | [extracted or "not addressed"] | [Strong / Weak / Missing] |
| Why 4 | Why would it work? | [extracted or "not addressed"] | [Strong / Weak / Missing] |
| Why 5 | Why this venue? | [extracted or "not addressed"] | [Strong / Weak / Missing] |

**Root Motivation:** [the deepest solid justification found, or "no solid root identified"]

**Gaps:** [list motivation levels that are weak or missing]

**Hard Questions:**
1. [question targeting the weakest link]
2. [question targeting second weakest link]
3. [optional third question if needed]
</output_format>

<success_criteria>
- Each "why" genuinely deepens the inquiry, not restating the prior level
- Weak and missing motivations are flagged honestly, not softened
- Hard questions are specific to this proposal, not generic research advice
- Analysis is grounded in what the researcher actually said, not assumed context
- Root motivation is identified or its absence is stated directly
</success_criteria>
